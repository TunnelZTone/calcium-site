Privacy Policy for CalcIUM

Last updated: March 31, 2026

CalcIUM is a scientific and engineering calculator application.

This Privacy Policy explains what data CalcIUM collects, how it is used, and what choices users have.

1. Data Collected by the App

CalcIUM is designed to work primarily on the device.

The app does not require account registration or user login.

The app does not use advertising SDKs.

The app does not collect or transmit personal information such as name, email address, phone number, contacts, location, microphone data, camera data, or photos for profiling or advertising purposes.

2. Local Data Storage

CalcIUM stores certain app data locally on the user’s device in order to provide core functionality. This may include:

  • calculation history
  • calculator settings
  • themes and interface preferences
  • graph history and graph-related settings
  • other app configuration data necessary for normal operation

This local data is stored only on the device unless the user explicitly exports or shares content.

3. Crash Reporting

CalcIUM uses Firebase Crashlytics to collect crash reports and technical diagnostics when the app crashes or encounters serious errors.

Crash reports may include technical information such as:

  • device and operating system information
  • app version
  • crash logs and stack traces
  • diagnostic keys used for troubleshooting, such as screen, theme, calculator mode, or overlay state

This information is used only to diagnose errors, improve app stability, and fix bugs.

CalcIUM does not use Firebase Analytics.

4. Internet and Network Use

CalcIUM is designed to work offline for its core calculator functionality.

If network communication occurs, it is only for technical service purposes such as crash reporting through Firebase Crashlytics. CalcIUM does not use its own backend server for user accounts, cloud sync, or remote data storage.

5. Sharing and Export

CalcIUM may allow the user to export or share certain content by explicit user action.

Currently, sharing is implemented for graph-related content, such as exported graph images or graph data files.

Any export or sharing action is initiated by the user. CalcIUM does not automatically send user-created content to external services.

6. Permissions

CalcIUM may request only limited permissions required for app functionality. At the current stage, the app uses vibration-related functionality for interface feedback.

CalcIUM does not request camera, microphone, contacts, or location access.

7. Purchases

CalcIUM may offer optional premium features in the future through Google Play in-app purchases.

If in-app purchases are enabled, payment processing is handled by Google Play, not directly by CalcIUM. CalcIUM does not store users’ full payment card details.

This Privacy Policy may be updated when such features become active.

8. Children

CalcIUM is a general-purpose calculator application and does not knowingly collect personal data from children.

9. Data Security

Because CalcIUM stores most operational data locally on the device and does not use user accounts or cloud storage, the amount of transmitted data is limited.

Reasonable efforts are made to keep the app stable and secure.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect app changes, legal requirements, or new functionality.

The updated version will be published with a new “Last updated” date.

11. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, you may contact the developer at:
